FRC Team 687
The Nerd Herd

"Be a nerd! Join the herd!"

"The Mission of Team 687, the Nerd Herd, is to encourage the youth to pursue fields in science and technology by offering an exciting mentor-based program that helps build real world engineering and business skills along with well-rounded life capabilities including self-confidence, communication, and leadership."

Our Mission

The Team

Established in 2001, Team 687 is composed of roughly 100 students from the California Academy of Mathematics and Science, a small, public high school in Carson, California. The team is heavily student-led, and competes in multiple competitions each year.

Mantis

Mantis is the Stronghold 2016 robot for Team 687, the Nerd Herd. Its superior ball handling and reliable drivebase make for an effective scoring combination.

Scorpion

In addition to Mantis, a second robot, Scorpion, was built to allow our drive team to practice with the new design while the mechanical and programming subteams could develop and test the new articulation.

Awards

Most recently, Team 687 was ranked 26th at the 2016 Las Vegas Regional. In the 2014 season, the team was a Regional Finalist at Las Vegas, and was able to compete at World Championship by earning a Wild Card slot. Team 687 also received the Entrepreneurship Award at the Las Vegas Regional in 2014. In the past, the team has won the Engineering Inspiration Award, the Woodie Flowers Award, the Website Award, the Sportsmanship Award, and numerous more Entrepreneurship Awards.

Facilities

The Nerd Herd is located on the CAMS Campus in Carson, California. The team has access to two CNC mills and two CNC lathes. The team meets on Mondays and Fridays from 4-7 and Saturdays from 9-3:30 at the CAMS Machine shop.
